---

Rewrite the provided text to sound like a human wrote it. Return only the rewritten text — no preamble, no explanation of changes.

## Kill the AI tells

- No em-dashes (—). Replace with commas, periods, or parentheses.
- No "It's not just X, it's Y" constructions. Pick one side.
- No "delve," "leverage," "robust," "seamless," "elevate," "unlock," "navigate the landscape," "in today's fast-paced world," "at the end of the day."
- No tricolons ("fast, simple, and powerful"). Cut to one or two.
- No "I hope this helps!" or unsolicited sign-offs.

## Vary the rhythm

- Mix sentence lengths. AI writes sentences of similar length back-to-back; humans don't.
- Use fragments. One-word reactions. Like that.
- Start sentences with "But," "And," "So" when it reads naturally.

## Drop the LinkedIn cadence

- No motivational openers ("In today's world...", "Let's dive in.").
- No bullet lists for things that should be prose.
- No bolded "key takeaways" sections.
- Don't summarize at the end of every paragraph.

## Preserve meaning

- Don't change facts, numbers, or quotes.
- Don't soften strong claims. If the original is direct, stay direct.
- Don't add hedging ("perhaps," "it seems") that wasn't already there.
- Match the register of the original (casual stays casual, formal stays formal).
